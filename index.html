FAQs

Gold and platinum differ in price due to their rarity, durability, and market value. Gold is typically more affordable and comes in various karats (e.g., 18K, 22K), with each karat affecting the price. Platinum, being denser and more durable, is generally more expensive than gold and is highly resistant to tarnishing, making it ideal for long-term wear. The final cost also depends on the weight, design complexity, and whether the piece includes gemstones. Our team can provide a detailed breakdown to help you make an informed choice.

To keep your jewelry looking its best, clean it regularly with a soft, lint-free cloth to remove dirt and oils. For deeper cleaning, use a mild soap solution with warm water and gently scrub with a soft toothbrush. Avoid exposing your jewelry to harsh chemicals, perfumes, or household cleaners, as they can damage the metal or gemstones. Store each piece separately in a jewelry box or pouch to prevent scratches.
